Depression vs Anxiety

The symptoms of depression can be really similar to those of anxiety.
It is realy important to identify the difference between these two.


Anxiety

Anxiety is more than just feeling stressed or worried. While stress and anxious feelings are a common response to a situation where we feel under pressure, they usually pass once the stressful situation has passed, or ‘stressor’ is removed.
Anxiety is when these anxious feelings don't go away – when they're ongoing and happen without any particular reason or cause. It’s a serious condition that makes it hard to cope with daily life. Everyone feels anxious from time to time, but for someone experiencing anxiety, these feelings aren't easily controlled.


Depression

While we all feel sad, moody or low from time to time, some people experience these feelings intensely, for long periods of time (weeks, months or even years) and sometimes without any apparent reason. Depression is more than just a low mood – it's a serious condition that affects your physical and mental health.
During this period, the person suffering from depression usually feels like isolating themselves from the rest of the world and feels the tendancy to harm themself or commit suicide.


Helping yourself through depression

1) Reaching Out

When one is depressed, they have a tendancy to isolate themselves from the world around them. Even reaching out to close friends and family can be really difficult. Social interaction is a huge step in battling depression, so even if you do not feel like talking about what is troubling you at the moment, talking about any topic is a good start.
Talking to your pets and taking care of them can also help a great deal in letting your thoughts out and easing the pain on your mind. 
Seeking help does not mean that you’re weak, it shows that you have the courage to accept what’s going on and work towards fixing it. 

2) Exercise

Exercise is a powerful depression fighter. During depression, you may not feel like getting out of bed, but making the effort to exercise will give a serious boost to your mood. Research has shown that 30 minutes of exercise a day proves as effective medication to battle depression symptoms, specially of fatigue.
The exercise can be anything from brisk walking to dancing to weight lifting. Find the right exercise for you and make it a part of your routine.
This would also give you a chance to connect with nature which is known to help people relax and improve their mood drastically.


3) Do things that you like

You will be surprised to see how much better you feel when you start doing things you like. 
Express yourself creatively through writting, music, art etc...
Take a day trip with friends to a museum or a park.
Pick up a former hobby or sport.


4) Take care of your health

Taking care of your health is extremely important and is most probably being neglected by you.
Get 8 hours of sleep.
being exposed to sunlight for atleast 15 minutes a day.
Eat healthy. Fruits like banana contain natural anti depressants.
challenge negative thinking and meditate for 5-10 minutes daily.


3 Week Checklist

The 3 week checklist is a method of slowly changing your habits and daily routine over the course of 3 weeks.
Every day, you have to complete the tasks given in the checklist. After the week is over, you move to the tasks in the next week, but also keep doing the tasks from the previous week(s).

Week 1

  • Went outside for atleast 15 minutes of sunlight

  • Ate one or more super food such as banana

  • talked with a family member or friend

  • Exercised atleast 30 minutes

Week 2

  • Practiced one relaxation technique

  • Challenged my negative thoughts

  • Avoided unhealthy and high sugar food

  • took care of my sleep pattern

Week 3

  • Start setting small, realistic goals for yourself

  • Spend more time doing something you love

  • Go out and spend time with someone

  • Spend 10 minutes at night reflecting your day

Different approach

List of 5

I was on the verge of letting go of everything, when I decided to talk to a friend about what I was feeling. She made me realise how stupid I was being and told me to think about what would my family go through if i gave up.
She told me to do 2 things that changed my life:

1) Go to the mirror and smile no matter how hard it is.

2) Make a list of 5 things I loved the most and stick it in a place I would see it every day.

It took some time, but things became so much better than before. 

Box

During my hard times, I started writting letters to myself and put them in a box with some other objects(such as photos and memories) which made me feel happy.
Whenever i was feeling low, I opened this box and read one of the letters and took out one of the objects.
This reminded me that there was a point when things were better and will be in the future also, once the storm has passed. 

Exercise

All my friends used to tease me because i was overweight and called me rude names, due to which I started isolating myself from them. Soon due to lonliness I started feeling low every day. I spoke to one of my neighbours’ about this and he told me that exercising would help me a lot and he would also join me.
We started running and he motivated me every day for the next month. The first few days I could not go even 200 metres without stopping, but gradually my stamina increased and now I can do 1500 metres without stopping.
I joined the gym and got a trainer who kept pushing me to do just slightly more than I did the previous day. Within 2 months I have become noticeably thinner and now my friends also keep encouraging me.
